Daily Journal

Mar 19, 2026

I just saw a video clip showing the rape of a Palestinian detainee by Israeli soldiers. He had rectal injuries, broken ribs, and a punctured lung. My head started spinning, my lips dried. The firs thing I did was pray for him. I felt bad beyond measure. Then I thought about oppression and crimes against humanity through the ages. Of course, the classic Islamic example is the Pharaoh of Egypt and his oppression of the twelve tribes of Israel. The word that God uses are mostly related to ultra forms of oppression but we never get to experience it, you know. There are no explicit mentions of rapes or burning or slipping open living mothers wombs or clicking a pregnant mom till the foetus dies inside the mother or staking or any of the bazillion atrocities that humans have created to afflict pain to another human (In case you are wondering, I didn't made these up, these are actual documented war crimes committed by armies across the world through the ages but mostly the US army, for more, see How the World Works by Noam Chomsky) . My point is it is not possible for us to feel what they are going through unless there's a certain level of depth of description. The best way to emphatise is to be in that person's place and experience those exact things which is impossible. The next best thing are words. So, the Quran doesn't do a very good job of getting the magnitude or extent of the Pharoah (or any of the other oppressors) atrocities across.1 And then it got me looking around and seeing all these poeple eating and chilling at a lounge at an airport in New Delhi. These people don't know about the pain and suffering of an oppressed people. They never will. How can humanity be ever capable of large-scale empathy? It's not possible. I think that's why the invention of God is necessary. An all-powerful, all-privileged entity who sets the rules. And then imagine if tomorrow the Muslims or any group came to power, the way the Jews are all powerful today - would the Muslims exercise empathy over the Jews? Actually, even forget about religion and labels. Would the top 0.0001% of the most rich who hold political and military sway ever care about the rest of humanity?

What are the core questions of philosophy? What is the purpose of man? What is the relation of one man to another man? Can we ever be a utopia? Like as in Plato's Utopia? If we study history then post WW2 has actually been one of the most peaceful times relative to other ages in human history. Then, in the "larger scheme of things", should we dismiss the Israel-Palestine conflict as a rough edge or an abberation where the larger section of humanity is living in peace? Or is the act of rape against even just one helpless man is justification enough to take to the streets and demand an overhaul of our political and social systems until there is no oppression? This, of course, raises the question of whether the state of no oppression is ever possible. Much ink has been spilt on this topic but you see it's a question about a possible scenario. The thing is - these questions do not have a self-qualifying answer. You can just never know unless you do it. You either do it or fail trying. Unless all possible ways of trying to achieve X doesn't fail, we cannot rule out the possibility that X is, in fact, possible. And do we really even **need** to get into so many questions? Is it not enough to stop an action knowing if it hurts even just one person beyond a justifiable level? Shouldn't philosophy be simple and as simple as possible. It is not to argue whether the Form of objects exist in the other Realm, it is to ensure dignity and prosperity of humanity. Philosophy should be functional. Functionalism.
